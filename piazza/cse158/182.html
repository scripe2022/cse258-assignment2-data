The main point of reference here would be to look at the part of the regression video where we explained why the MSE was generally a good choice. Basically, if errors follow a normal distribution. A case where this is often violated is a dataset that has significant outliers -- since you're penalizing the *square* of the error, outliers can result in massive penalties and hence cause you to fit a bad model. The MAE doesn't suffer from this problem, since we wouldn't be penalizing the square of the error. But just as we showed that the MSE corresponds to a specific (Gaussian) error distribution, the MAE corresponds to a different one (I think Laplace or something, which just allows the error distribution to be long-tailed).

\n

\n

The midterm question only sorta hints at it, but indeed this is a dataset with big outliers, hence why the MAE would be a good choice of error metric.
