You can use any models you like! But would generally advise:

\n
  • The vast majority of solutions that get full marks will be methods I've taught in class.
  • Usually the top solution is also a careful implementation of a method from class. It is very rare that the top solution is some fancypants deep learning thing (though by definition the "top" solution is usually something a bit weird).
  • I would strongly suggest you try "easy" models (like similarity functions) before attempting any of the fancier models (even among models from class).
\n

The structure of the assignment is such that it's a fairly "pure" recommender systems task -- it is pretty hard to apply types of ML from elsewhere.
